## Mississippi-Specific Construction Law

### Contractor Licensing

- Mississippi State Board of Contractors (MSBOC) regulates contractors
- License required for any residential or commercial project over **$10,000**
- Contractors must carry a **surety bond** (amount varies by classification, typically $10,000-$25,000)
- Working without a license on a project over $10,000 is a misdemeanor, punishable by fine and/or imprisonment
- An unlicensed contractor **cannot enforce a contract** in Mississippi courts — they lose the right to sue for payment

### Mechanic's Liens

- Mississippi Code 85-7-131 through 85-7-145 governs construction liens
- **Who can file:** Contractors, subcontractors, laborers, and material suppliers who provide work or materials for improving real property
- **Filing deadline:** Lien must be filed within **12 months** of the last date work was performed or materials were supplied
- **Notice requirement:** No preliminary notice required for general contractors. Subcontractors and material suppliers must provide a **Notice to Owner** within 30 days of first furnishing labor/materials to preserve lien rights.
- **Enforcement:** Lien must be enforced (lawsuit filed) within **180 days** of filing the lien. If not enforced, the lien expires.
- **Homestead exemption:** Mississippi homestead exemptions do NOT protect against mechanic's liens. A valid lien can force a sale of the homeowner's primary residence.
- **FTW implication:** Milestone payments through QuickBooks significantly reduce lien risk because contractors are paid progressively. A contractor who has been paid through 4 of 5 milestones has little financial incentive to file a lien.

### Statute of Limitations for Construction Claims

| Claim Type | Time Limit | Mississippi Code |
|------------|-----------|-----------------|
| Breach of construction contract | 3 years (oral) / 6 years (written) | 15-1-29 / 15-1-49 |
| Defective workmanship (negligence) | 3 years from discovery | 15-1-49 |
| Fraud | 3 years from discovery | 15-1-67 |
| Statute of repose (new construction) | 6 years from substantial completion | 15-1-41 |
| Mechanic's lien filing | 12 months from last work performed | 85-7-141 |

### Contractor Bond Requirements

- MSBOC requires a surety bond for all licensed contractors
- Bond amounts by classification:
  - Residential Builder: $10,000
  - Commercial Builder: $10,000-$25,000 (varies by project size)
  - Specialty trades (Electrical, Plumbing, HVAC): $10,000
- Bonds protect homeowners if a contractor fails to complete work or violates the contract
- Homeowner can file a claim against the contractor's bond through MSBOC
- **FTW implication:** Verified contractors on FTW have confirmed active bonds. This is an additional layer of homeowner protection that FTW should communicate clearly.

## FTW's Dispute Resolution Process

### Step 1: Complaint Filed

- Either party (contractor or homeowner) files a dispute through the FTW platform
- Dispute form collects: project ID, nature of complaint (dropdown categories matching the top 10 list above), description of issue, supporting evidence (photos, messages, documents), desired resolution
- Filing a dispute immediately pauses any pending milestone payments for the project
- Both parties are notified that a dispute has been opened

### Step 2: Both Parties Respond

- The non-filing party has **5 business days** to submit their response
- Response includes: their version of events, supporting evidence, proposed resolution
- All project documentation on FTW (messages, estimates, milestone history, photos) is automatically pulled into the dispute record
- If the non-filing party does not respond within 5 business days, the dispute proceeds based on available evidence

### Step 3: AI-Assisted Review

- FTW's system performs an automated preliminary review:
  - Compare the original AI estimate to the contractor's bid (was the bid reasonable?)
  - Review milestone payment history (what has been paid, what is outstanding?)
  - Check project timeline against original estimate (is the delay unreasonable?)
  - Flag any patterns (has this contractor had previous disputes?)
- This review generates a summary for the human mediator, not a binding decision

### Step 4: Mediation

- A FTW team member reviews the dispute file (both responses, evidence, AI summary)
- Mediator contacts both parties (via platform messaging or phone) to discuss resolution
- Mediation focuses on practical outcomes: partial refund, completion of remaining work, revised timeline, milestone adjustment
- Target resolution time: **10 business days** from complaint filing
- Most disputes (estimated 70-80%) should resolve at this stage through compromise

### Step 5: Resolution

- Mediator proposes a resolution. Both parties accept or reject.
- If both accept: resolution is documented, milestone payments are adjusted accordingly, dispute is closed
- If the resolution involves a refund: processed through QuickBooks (FTW does not hold funds, so refunds go through the original payment channel)
- Resolution is noted on both parties' accounts (not publicly visible, but tracked internally for pattern detection)

### Step 6: Appeal (If Resolution Rejected)

- If either party rejects the mediation outcome, they can appeal within **7 business days**
- Appeal is reviewed by a senior FTW team member (or external mediator if the amount exceeds $5,000)
- Appeal decision is final within the FTW platform
- If the dispute remains unresolved after appeal, FTW provides both parties with:
  - Documentation package (all evidence, messages, payment records)
  - Information about Mississippi small claims court (jurisdiction up to $3,500)
  - Information about MSBOC complaint process (for licensed contractor violations)
  - Recommendation to consult a construction attorney
- FTW's role ends here. The platform is not a court and does not make legally binding decisions.

## QuickBooks Milestone Payments: How They Reduce Risk

### The Problem with Traditional Payment

Traditional construction payment: homeowner pays 50% upfront ($25,000 on a $50,000 project), contractor starts work, homeowner pays the remaining 50% at completion. If the contractor abandons the project after receiving the deposit, the homeowner loses $25,000 and has an incomplete kitchen.

### FTW's Milestone Payment Model

Example: $50,000 kitchen remodel broken into 5 milestones via QuickBooks progress invoicing.

| Milestone | Description | Amount | Payment Trigger |
|-----------|-------------|--------|-----------------|
| 1 | Demolition complete, rough framing inspected | $7,500 (15%) | Homeowner approves + photo evidence |
| 2 | Plumbing and electrical rough-in complete | $10,000 (20%) | Homeowner approves + inspection passed |
| 3 | Cabinets installed, countertop templated | $12,500 (25%) | Homeowner approves + photo evidence |
| 4 | Countertops installed, tile complete, fixtures set | $12,500 (25%) | Homeowner approves + photo evidence |
| 5 | Final punch list complete, final inspection passed | $7,500 (15%) | Homeowner approves + walkthrough |

### Why This Works

- **Maximum exposure at any point: $12,500** (the largest single milestone), not $25,000+
- **If contractor abandons after Milestone 2:** Homeowner has paid $17,500 for $17,500 worth of completed, inspected work. They can hire a new contractor to finish from that point.
- **If workmanship is defective at Milestone 3:** Homeowner withholds Milestone 3 payment and files a dispute. Contractor has incentive to fix the work to unlock payment.
- **Change orders are handled per-milestone.** If scope changes after Milestone 2, the remaining milestones are re-quoted and both parties agree before work continues.
- **QuickBooks generates invoices automatically** for each milestone. Both parties have financial records. No "he said, she said" about what was paid.

## Insurance Coverage for Dispute-Related Claims

### Contractor's General Liability Insurance

- Covers property damage and bodily injury caused by the contractor's work
- If a contractor damages a homeowner's property (e.g., breaks a water main during excavation), the contractor's GL policy should cover the repair
- FTW verifies that contractors carry minimum $500,000 GL coverage
- Homeowners can file a claim directly with the contractor's insurance carrier (FTW provides carrier info from the verified COI on file)

### Contractor's Surety Bond

- Covers failure to perform contract obligations
- If a contractor takes payment and doesn't complete the work, the homeowner can file a bond claim through MSBOC
- Bond amounts are limited ($10,000-$25,000), so they don't cover large losses on big projects
- FTW's milestone payment system reduces the gap between bond coverage and actual exposure

### Homeowner's Options When Insurance Doesn't Cover It

- File a complaint with MSBOC (can result in contractor license revocation)
- Mississippi small claims court (up to $3,500)
- County circuit court (claims above $3,500)
- Consult a construction attorney (FTW can provide referrals but does not offer legal advice)

## AI Estimate as Independent Price Benchmark

### How It Works

ConstructionAI generates estimates using regional pricing data, material costs, and labor rates specific to the project's location. When a homeowner posts a job and receives bids from contractors, the AI estimate serves as an independent baseline.

### Red Flag Detection

| Scenario | AI Estimate | Contractor Bid | Flag |
|----------|------------|----------------|------|
| Normal range | $45,000 | $48,000 | No flag (within 10%) |
| High bid | $45,000 | $72,000 | Warning: "This bid is 60% above the AI estimate" |
| Suspiciously low | $45,000 | $22,000 | Warning: "This bid is 51% below the AI estimate — may indicate cut corners or hidden costs" |
| Price gouging | $45,000 | $135,000 | Alert: "This bid is 3x the AI estimate" |

### How This Reduces Disputes

- **Prevents overpayment disputes.** Homeowners who see an AI estimate of $45K won't unknowingly agree to a $90K bid without being warned.
- **Prevents unrealistic expectations.** Homeowners who expect a $15K kitchen remodel when the AI estimates $45K are corrected before they even post the job.
- **Provides dispute evidence.** If a contractor bids $50K, completes half the work, and demands another $40K for "unforeseen costs," the AI estimate showing $50K total serves as evidence that the additional charges are unreasonable.
- **Levels the information asymmetry.** Construction pricing is opaque to most homeowners. The AI estimate gives them a reference point, reducing the power imbalance that leads to disputes.
