# Three Routes to Market

Three distinct business models FTW can pursue. Each stands alone, but they can also be sequenced — start with one, layer the next when the first is proven.

---

## Route A: Vertical SaaS (Tools-First, Marketplace Later)

**The ServiceTitan Play.** Build the best contractor tools platform for residential construction. Marketplace is a feature, not the core product.

### How It Works
1. Launch as a free/paid SaaS tool suite for contractors: estimation (ConstructionAI), project management, invoicing, scheduling, QuickBooks sync
2. Contractors sign up for the tools, not for leads
3. Once contractor base is established (50-100 in a region), layer on homeowner-facing marketplace as a feature
4. Revenue comes from subscriptions and QB payment processing revenue share — NOT lead fees or transaction fees

### Revenue Model
| Stream | Monthly | Notes |
|--------|---------|-------|
| Free tier | $0 | Estimation, basic project management |
| Pro tier | $49/mo | ConstructionAI Estimate Agent, advanced features |
| Business tier | $149/mo | Team management, analytics, priority support |
| QB rev share | ~$5-15/mo per contractor | 20% of payment processing for 3 years |
| **Target: 200 Pro contractors** | **$9,800+/mo** | |

### Why This Route
- **No cold-start problem.** Contractors sign up for tools, not a marketplace. You don't need homeowners day one.
- **ServiceTitan proved this model.** $961M revenue, $9B valuation, IPO'd. They started with tools for HVAC/plumbing, not a marketplace.
- **Vertical SaaS has 110-120% net revenue retention** — expansion revenue from existing customers exceeds churn. Horizontal SaaS only gets 102-105%.
- **Easier to sell.** "Free tools for your business" is an easier pitch than "join our marketplace."
- **QuickBooks integration is the moat.** Once contractors' financial workflows run through FTW→QB, switching cost is massive.

### Risks
- Slower to build network effects — no marketplace flywheel until later
- Competing against established tools (Buildertrend, Jobber, CoConstruct)
- Must nail product quality because tools ARE the product, not a side feature

### Expansion Path
Oxford → Tupelo/Memphis → Nashville → broader Southeast. Each market starts with contractor tool adoption, marketplace layers on after density.

### Key Metric
**Monthly Active Tool Users.** Not marketplace transactions. Contractors using estimation, invoicing, and project management weekly.

## Route B: Marketplace-First (Two-Sided Platform)

**The Thumbtack Play.** Build the marketplace. Match contractors with homeowners. Revenue from platform participation.

### How It Works
1. Recruit 10-15 Oxford contractors through personal relationships
2. Simultaneously seed homeowner demand through local channels (Facebook, NextDoor, church networks, real estate agents)
3. Homeowners post jobs, contractors bid, platform facilitates matching
4. Revenue from contractor subscriptions + homeowner convenience fees + featured placement

### Revenue Model
| Stream | Monthly | Notes |
|--------|---------|-------|
| Contractor Pro | $49/mo | Tools + marketplace participation |
| Homeowner fee | $4.99/job | Platform protection + verified contractors |
| Featured placement | $29-99/mo | Priority in search results |
| QB rev share | ~$5-15/mo per contractor | 20% of payment processing |
| AI estimates | $5-15/each | Or bundled in Pro |
| **Target: 100 contractors + 500 homeowners** | **~$7,000/mo** | |

### Why This Route
- **Network effects compound.** More contractors → better homeowner experience → more homeowners → more contractors want in.
- **Marketplace revenue scales non-linearly.** Once liquidity is achieved, each new user adds value for all existing users.
- **Addresses the trust gap directly.** 30% of homeowners delay projects because they can't find trusted pros. Marketplace with verification solves this.
- **FTW's zero-lead-fee model is genuinely differentiated.** Contractors pay $135/lead on Angi. FTW charges $0 per lead + $49/mo subscription.

### Risks
- **Cold-start problem is real.** Need both sides simultaneously. 90% of two-sided marketplace apps fail in year one.
- **Disintermediation.** After first job, contractor and homeowner exchange numbers and go direct. (Mitigated by subscription model — no per-transaction fee to avoid.)
- **Requires marketing budget for demand side.** Tools are supply-side; homeowner acquisition needs separate investment.
- **Oxford is small.** 27,448 people. May not have enough transaction volume to prove the model.

### Expansion Path
Must prove Oxford density (minimum 10 active contractors, 5+ jobs/week flowing through platform) before expanding. Memphis or Tupelo next.

### Key Metric
**Jobs Completed Through Platform Per Week.** Not signups. Actual completed jobs that went through FTW from posting to payment.

## Route C: Data & API Platform (ConstructionAI as the Product)

**The RSMeans/Gordian Play.** Sell construction estimation intelligence as a service. FTW becomes the data layer that other platforms build on.

### How It Works
1. Package ConstructionAI as an API — other construction apps, lenders, insurance companies, and real estate platforms pay for estimation data
2. White-label the estimation platform to contractors (FairEstimator → MHPEstimate model, already proven)
3. License regional pricing data (built from real contractor estimates) to industry players
4. FTW consumer marketplace becomes one customer of the API, not the entire business

### Revenue Model
| Stream | Monthly | Notes |
|--------|---------|-------|
| White-label licensing | $200-500/mo per client | MHP is first client, scale to 10-50 |
| API access (usage-based) | $0.05-$0.50/estimate | Per-request pricing for external apps |
| API subscription tier | $500-2,000/mo | Unlimited estimates for enterprise clients |
| Data licensing | $1,000-5,000/mo | Regional pricing databases to lenders, insurers |
| **Target: 20 white-label + API customers** | **$15,000+/mo** | |

### Why This Route
- **No cold-start problem at all.** Each client is independent. No network effects needed.
- **RSMeans (Gordian) proves the model.** They sell construction cost data — 92,000 line items across 970 locations. Annual subscriptions. Massive business.
- **ConstructionAI is the moat.** 5,200 real training examples, 28,941 line items from actual Mississippi projects. No competitor has this quality of regional data.
- **Multiple customer types.** Contractors (white-label), other SaaS platforms (API), lenders (risk assessment), insurance (property valuation), real estate (renovation cost estimation).
- **Highest margin.** API calls cost fractions of a penny ($0.0003-$0.0015 per estimate). Selling at $0.05-$0.50 = 30-1600x markup.
- **Already proven.** MHP Construction is paying for a white-label deployment. This isn't theoretical.

### Risks
- **Smaller addressable market per customer** than consumer marketplace (fewer customers, higher revenue each)
- **Requires sales capability.** B2B sales cycle is longer than consumer signups.
- **Data must stay accurate.** If ConstructionAI estimates are wrong, clients leave. Need continuous training data pipeline.
- **Regional data gaps.** Currently strong in Mississippi. Expanding to other states requires new training data.

### Expansion Path
Mississippi white-label clients → Southeast API customers → National data licensing → Integration partnerships (Zillow, Redfin, insurance companies want renovation cost estimates).

### Key Metric
**API Calls Per Month + White-Label Clients.** Revenue is directly tied to usage volume and client count.

## Recommended Sequence: A → C → B

### Phase 1: Vertical SaaS (Route A) — Months 1-6
Launch with contractor tools. Free estimation, project management, invoicing with QuickBooks sync. Get 50-100 contractors in Oxford using the tools daily. No marketplace yet — just prove the tools are valuable enough that contractors pay $49/mo.

**Why first:** Easiest to launch. No cold start. One-sided acquisition. Proves product-market fit with the supply side.

### Phase 2: Data & API (Route C) — Months 3-12 (overlapping)
While building contractor base, simultaneously grow white-label and API business. Sign 10-20 white-label clients (other contractors who want branded estimation platforms). Open ConstructionAI API to external developers. License pricing data.

**Why second:** Already proven with MHP. Generates revenue without needing marketplace. Funds the marketplace build.

### Phase 3: Marketplace (Route B) — Months 6-18
Once contractor density is proven (50+ active tool users in Oxford), layer on the homeowner marketplace. Contractors are already on the platform using tools — now add the demand side. Marketing budget funded by Route A subscriptions + Route C API revenue.

**Why last:** Hardest to launch. Requires both sides. But by this point you have contractors already on the platform, revenue funding marketing, and proof that the tools work.

### The Beauty of This Sequence
- **Route A generates contractors** (supply side for eventual marketplace)
- **Route C generates revenue** (funds marketplace marketing)
- **Route B creates the flywheel** (network effects compound everything)
- **If Route B never works, Routes A + C are still viable standalone businesses**
- **You never bet everything on the marketplace working**

## What ServiceTitan Teaches Us

ServiceTitan is the proof case for Route A → B sequencing:
- **Started as SaaS tools** for HVAC/plumbing contractors
- **Revenue: $961M** (FY2026), growing 24% YoY
- **Valuation: $9B** at IPO
- **Model: 70% subscription, 25% usage, 5% services**
- **$62B in gross transaction volume** flows through their platform
- **They did NOT start as a marketplace.** They started as tools. The marketplace (connecting homeowners to contractors through their platform) came later, after contractors were already dependent on the tools.

FTW can follow this exact playbook at a fraction of the scale, in a specific vertical (residential construction estimation), in a specific geography (Mississippi → Southeast).

## Route D: Mississippi Blitz (Aggressive Growth)

See [route-d-blitz.md](route-d-blitz.md) for the full plan.

**The Play:** Capture 1,000 of Mississippi's ~10,600 licensed contractors in 12 months. Flood homeowners with ads. Lock the state before any competitor can enter.

- **12-month investment:** ~$67K (covered 4x by SBIR)
- **Month 12 MRR:** $27,750 ($333K ARR)
- **Channels:** Field reps, HBA partnerships, Facebook/Instagram ads, Google LSA, NextDoor, referral program, brand ambassadors, content/SEO, trade shows
- **Break-even:** Month 14-15
- **Moat:** Data, network density, switching costs, brand recognition, association partnerships, regulatory knowledge, first-mover in a small market

Route D is the accelerated version of A→C→B. Same sequence, but with marketing spend and sales reps compressing the timeline from 18 months to 12. Requires SBIR or equivalent funding to execute.
