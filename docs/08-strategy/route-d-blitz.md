# Route D: Mississippi Blitz — 1,000 Contractors, Statewide Dominance

**Goal:** Capture 10% of Mississippi's ~10,600 licensed contractors within 12 months, then flood homeowners with ads. Burn the market so no competitor can enter Mississippi without fighting FTW.

This is the aggressive play. Not for the faint of heart. Requires either SBIR funding, investor capital, or revenue from Routes A/C to fund it.

## Defensive Moat: Why Competitors Can't Follow

Once FTW has 1,000 Mississippi contractors:

1. **Data moat.** 1,000 contractors generating estimates = thousands of real pricing data points per month. ConstructionAI gets smarter with every estimate. No competitor can replicate this data without building the same user base.

2. **Network density.** In a small state, 1,000 contractors means most homeowners can find a verified contractor nearby. This is the threshold where marketplace effects kick in.

3. **Switching cost.** Contractors' financial workflows run through FTW→QuickBooks. Historical estimates, project data, reviews, and verification badges all live on FTW. Switching means losing everything.

4. **Brand recognition.** After 12 months of content, ads, trade shows, and ambassador posts, "FairTradeWorker" becomes synonymous with "find a contractor in Mississippi."

5. **Association partnerships.** HBAM, HBAJ, HBA Coast, HBANMS, ABC Mississippi. These relationships take time to build. A competitor entering Mississippi would need to win them over from scratch.

6. **Regulatory knowledge.** FTW's MSBOC integration, Mississippi-specific pricing data, and local compliance workflows are hard to replicate from outside the state.

7. **First-mover advantage in a small market.** Mississippi is not a market that attracts VC-backed competitors. Thumbtack barely penetrates it. Angi is weak here. FTW can own it because nobody else is trying to.

## Risk Mitigation

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|
| Burn rate exceeds revenue | SBIR covers 4x the needed burn. Without SBIR, reduce to 1 field rep and cut ad spend 50%. |
| Contractors sign up but don't use tools | Onboarding flow gets them to first estimate in < 10 minutes. Weekly "tips" emails. Ambassador check-ins. |
| Homeowner ads don't convert | Don't scale homeowner ads until contractor density is proven. Test $500/mo first, only scale what converts. |
| Field rep underperforms | Commission-heavy comp means low risk. If rep can't hit 15 signups/month, replace within 60 days. |
| Competitor enters Mississippi | Unlikely in Year 1. If it happens, data moat + association partnerships + switching costs protect you. |
| Cash runs out before break-even | Cut to Phase 1 costs only ($1,700/mo). Routes A and C are still viable without the blitz. |
| QB marketplace deadline (May 1) | Build QB integration in parallel. If missed, API access still works — just no revenue share until listed. |

## Decision Framework: When to Go Blitz

Pull the trigger on Route D when ANY of these are true:
- SBIR Phase 1 is awarded ($275K)
- InvestMS provides seed funding
- Route A (tools) has 50+ paying contractors and positive unit economics
- Route C (API/white-label) is generating $5K+/mo
- Dad's funding can cover $5,600/mo burn for 12 months

Do NOT go blitz if:
- Product isn't ready (bugs, missing features, poor onboarding)
- No paying contractors yet (prove the model first)
- Legal/compliance isn't sorted (TOS, insurance, FCRA)
- QuickBooks integration isn't working
