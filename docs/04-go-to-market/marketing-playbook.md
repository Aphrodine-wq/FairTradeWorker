# Marketing Playbook

## Contractor Acquisition (Supply Side)

### Phase 1: Personal Outreach (Oxford Launch)

- James personally recruits first 10-15 contractors
- Coffee meetings, not cold emails
- Pitch: "Free estimation tools. No lead fees. Ever."
- MHP/Josh as proof: "Your competitor is already using this."
- Target trades in order of priority: roofing, kitchen/bath remodel, painting, general handyman
- Track every conversation in a simple spreadsheet: name, trade, phone, status (called/met/signed up/active)

### Phase 2: Local Digital (Post-Launch)

- Facebook: Before/after project photos, job proof posts tagged to Oxford
- Instagram: Time-lapse videos, project transformations. Construction gets **4.4% engagement rate** on Instagram (highest of any industry)
- Content that works: Job proof > education posts > team spotlights > testimonials
- What doesn't work: Ads that look like ads. "Contractors don't want ads — they want proof, people, and expertise."

### Phase 3: SEO & Discovery

- Google Business Profile for FTW Oxford (map pack appears first for "contractor near me")
- Review signals = volume, recency, and rating quality drive local rankings
- Real project photos are critical — competitors lose rankings when they use stock images
- Each service category needs its own page (roofing, kitchen remodel, etc.)
- Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) — optimize for AI answers (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini), not just Google

## Homeowner Acquisition (Demand Side)

### Phase 1: Community Seeding

- Oxford Facebook community groups
- NextDoor (powerful for local home services)
- Ole Miss parent/alumni groups
- Real estate agent partnerships ("know a good contractor?" --> "check FTW")
- Church networks — Oxford has strong church communities and real word of mouth flows through them

### Phase 2: Content Marketing

- Homeowner education: "3 signs your roof needs attention," "What a kitchen remodel actually costs in Oxford"
- These posts are shareable in local Facebook groups — free distribution
- AI-generated estimates as content: "What does a bathroom remodel cost in Oxford, MS? Here's what ConstructionAI says."

### Phase 3: Paid (Only After Organic Works)

- Facebook/Instagram ads targeting Oxford homeowners
- Google Local Services Ads ($92/lead average — but FTW is the destination, not a competing lead-gen)
- Don't spend on paid until organic channels are validated

## Email Nurture Sequences

### Sequence 1: Contractor Signed Up Free, Hasn't Gone Pro

**Goal:** Get them using the free tools consistently, then present Pro as the logical upgrade.

| Day | Subject Line | Content |
|-----|-------------|---------|
| Day 0 | "Welcome to FairTradeWorker" | Account confirmation. Quick-start guide: complete your profile, run your first estimate, set your service area. |
| Day 2 | "Your first AI estimate is waiting" | Walk them through using ConstructionAI. Link directly to the estimation tool. Show a sample estimate for a common project in their trade. |
| Day 5 | "How [contractor name] landed 3 jobs last month" | Testimonial/case study from an active FTW contractor. Show the path: profile --> bid --> win job. |
| Day 10 | "Your profile is 60% complete" | Nudge to add photos, license info, insurance docs. "Complete profiles get 3x more homeowner views." |
| Day 15 | "Homeowners in Oxford are posting jobs" | Show them (anonymized) recent job posts in their trade/area. "You could have bid on these." |
| Day 21 | "What Pro contractors get" | Soft pitch for Pro: priority placement, advanced estimation, enhanced profile badge. No pressure — "upgrade when you're ready." |
| Day 30 | "Still on the free plan?" | Direct ask: "Is there something holding you back? Reply to this email and tell me — I read every one. -James" |

### Sequence 2: Homeowner Browsed But Didn't Post a Job

**Goal:** Get them to post their first project.

| Day | Subject Line | Content |
|-----|-------------|---------|
| Day 0 | "Welcome to FairTradeWorker" | Account confirmation. "Post your project and get bids from licensed contractors in Oxford." |

| Day 3 | "3 contractors in Oxford are ready for your project" | Show them 3 real contractor profiles in the trade they browsed (if known). "Post your project and they'll send you bids." |
| Day 7 | "Most homeowners get their first bid within 24 hours" | Address the fear: "You're not committing to anything by posting. See what comes back." |
| Day 14 | "Still thinking about that [project type]?" | Seasonal or practical angle: "Spring is peak season — contractors book up fast. Post now while availability is high." |

## Local PR Strategy

### Oxford Eagle (Local Newspaper)

- **Pitch angle:** "Oxford contractor builds tech platform to fix the construction industry"
- **Contact:** Submit to business/local section editor
- **Timing:** Pitch 2-3 weeks before desired publication date
- **What to include:** James's story (construction background, self-taught developer), how FTW helps Oxford homeowners, the local angle (built here, launching here)
- **Follow-up:** Offer to do a feature story with a local contractor using the platform

### Ole Miss Alumni Newsletter / The Ole Miss Alumni Association

- **Pitch angle:** "Ole Miss community member builds innovative construction platform"
- **Why it works:** Ole Miss alumni are a tight network. Many are homeowners in Oxford or have connections there.
- **Contact:** Alumni association communications team
- **Ask:** Feature in their digital newsletter or alumni spotlight

### Local Radio (Rebel Radio 92.1 FM / SuperTalk Mississippi)

- **Pitch angle:** Community interest segment — "Local entrepreneur modernizes home contracting"
- **Format:** 5-10 minute interview segment
- **Talking points:** What homeowners struggle with when hiring contractors, how FTW is different from Angi/Thumbtack, the free AI estimation tool
- **CTA on air:** "Visit FairTradeWorker.com — it's free for homeowners and free for contractors to get started"

### Mississippi Business Journal

- **Pitch angle:** Tech startup emerging from Mississippi's construction industry
- **Timing:** After first 50 jobs completed on the platform (need traction for this one)
- **What to include:** Revenue numbers if available, number of contractors/homeowners, growth metrics

### What Agents Get

- A personalized referral link to share with clients
- Peace of mind — they're not personally recommending a contractor, they're recommending a vetted platform
- Reduced liability vs. giving personal contractor recommendations
- Future: referral credit or co-marketing opportunities once FTW monetizes

### How to Find Agents

- Search Realtor.com for top-producing agents in Oxford/Lafayette County

- Attend a local real estate networking event
- Ask existing contacts: "Who's the busiest real estate agent you know in Oxford?"
- Target: 5-10 agent partnerships in the first 3 months
