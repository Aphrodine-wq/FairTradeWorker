FTW is a marketplace, NOT an employer, general contractor, or party to the construction contract. FTW facilitates connections only.

- FTW does not employ, supervise, direct, or control any contractor on the platform
- FTW is not a party to any agreement between homeowners and contractors — that relationship is exclusively between them
- FTW does not guarantee the quality, safety, legality, or completion of any construction work
- FTW does not provide construction advice, architectural services, or engineering guidance — ConstructionAI estimates are informational tools, not professional recommendations
- The FairTrade Promise describes platform standards and values — it does NOT create any warranty, guarantee, or legally enforceable promise regarding contractor performance

## Section 2: Registration and Accounts

Contractor requirements (license, insurance, background check). Homeowner requirements (address, identity).

- Contractors must provide: legal business name, valid Mississippi contractor license number (if applicable to their trade/project scope), proof of general liability insurance, consent to FCRA-compliant background check

- Account credentials must not be shared — each user is responsible for all activity under their account
- FTW reserves the right to deny registration or revoke accounts for providing false information, failing verification, or violating these terms

## Section 3: Contractor Obligations

Maintain valid license, insurance, and verification status. Complete work as bid. Respond to disputes.

- Contractors must maintain valid state licensure for the work they perform (where required by Mississippi law)
- Contractors must maintain general liability insurance at minimum levels set by FTW (currently $500K per occurrence)
- Contractors must notify FTW within 5 business days if their license or insurance lapses, is suspended, or is revoked
- Contractors are solely responsible for the quality, safety, and legality of all work they perform
- Contractors must respond to homeowner dispute claims within 5 business days of notification by FTW

## Section 4: Homeowner Obligations

Accurate project descriptions. Timely payment through QuickBooks. Good-faith dispute engagement.

- Homeowners must provide accurate project descriptions, including scope of work, property condition, and any known issues (asbestos, mold, structural concerns)
- Homeowners must make payments according to the agreed-upon schedule with the contractor, processed through QuickBooks Online
- Homeowners must engage in good faith with FTW's dispute resolution process before pursuing external legal action
- Homeowners must not solicit contractors through FTW for the purpose of obtaining free estimates with no intent to hire
- Homeowners are responsible for obtaining any required building permits for their projects

## Section 5: Payment Terms

Payments flow through QuickBooks to contractor directly. FTW does not hold, escrow, or process construction payments. FTW charges subscription fees separately.

- All construction payments are processed through QuickBooks Online and flow directly from homeowner to contractor. FTW does not hold funds at any point.
- FTW is not an escrow agent, payment processor, or financial intermediary for construction payments
- FTW subscription fees (Free, Pro, Enterprise tiers) are billed separately to contractors via QuickBooks and are non-refundable once the billing period has begun
- Homeowner platform access is free. Homeowners do not pay FTW any fees for posting jobs or receiving bids.
- FTW may earn revenue through Intuit/QuickBooks revenue sharing, materials referral partnerships, and other B2B channels — these do not add costs to homeowners or contractors

- Arbitration venue: Lafayette County, Mississippi
- Governing law: State of Mississippi
- The arbitrator's decision is final and binding, and judgment may be entered in any court of competent jurisdiction
- Small claims court: Either party may bring an individual action in small claims court in Lafayette County, Mississippi, as an alternative to arbitration for claims within the court's jurisdictional limit
