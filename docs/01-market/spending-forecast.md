## Seasonal Spending Patterns

### Q1 (January - March): Planning Season
- **15%** of annual spending — lowest quarter
- Homeowners researching, getting quotes, planning spring projects
- Contractors are bidding and scheduling for the busy season
- FTW opportunity: onboard contractors during their planning window

### Q2 (April - June): Peak Season
- **32%** of annual spending — highest quarter
- Exterior work dominates: roofing, siding, painting, landscaping, decks
- Contractors are fully booked; homeowners face longest wait times
- FTW opportunity: marketplace value is highest when demand exceeds supply

### Q3 (July - September): Sustained Activity
- **30%** of annual spending
- Interior remodels peak (too hot for exterior in the South)
- Back-to-school drives "finish before the kids come home" urgency
- Hurricane season creates surge demand in Southeast
- FTW opportunity: storm-response contractor matching

### Q4 (October - December): Wind-Down
- **23%** of annual spending
- Holiday-driven deadlines ("kitchen done by Thanksgiving")
- Year-end tax planning drives some commercial/rental property renovations
- Slowdown in December as homeowners focus on holidays
- FTW opportunity: "New Year, new home" marketing campaigns for January launch projects

### Mississippi-Specific Seasonal Note
- Mississippi's mild winters shift more spending into Q4 and Q1 compared to northern states
- Exterior work is viable nearly year-round in Mississippi (rare hard freezes)
- Hurricane season (June-November) creates unpredictable demand spikes — contractors who are set up on FTW can respond to urgent requests faster than those who rely on word-of-mouth alone

## Impact of Interest Rates on Renovation Spending

### Current Environment (2026)
- Mortgage rates: **6.2-6.8%** range (elevated compared to 2020-2021 lows of 2.5-3%)
- **Lock-in effect**: 85% of existing homeowners have mortgage rates below 5%. Moving means giving up a low rate, so they renovate instead.
- Home equity levels: **$35 trillion** nationally — record high. Homeowners have the equity to fund renovations.
- **HELOC activity** up **18%** YoY as homeowners tap equity for improvements

### Rate Impact Analysis
- Every **1% increase** in mortgage rates reduces home sales by **~8-12%** but **increases** renovation spending by **~3-5%** (lock-in effect)
- If rates drop below 5.5%, expect a **housing transaction surge** that temporarily reduces renovation spending but increases move-in renovation demand (new buyers fixing up purchases)
- FTW benefits from BOTH scenarios: high rates = more renovation, low rates = more move-in renovation. The platform wins regardless of rate direction.

## DIY vs. Professional Split

### Current Breakdown (2026)
- **Professional (contractor-performed)**: 62% of spending ($325B)
- **DIY**: 28% of spending ($147B)
- **Hybrid (homeowner manages, hires for specific tasks)**: 10% of spending ($52B)

### Trend Direction
- DIY share is **declining** for complex trades (electrical, plumbing, HVAC) due to code requirements and safety concerns
- DIY share is **flat** for cosmetic work (painting, landscaping, simple flooring)
- DIY share is **increasing slightly** for demolition and prep work (homeowners demo, contractors install)
- **Hybrid is the fastest-growing segment**: homeowners who want control but need professional execution for specific tasks. FTW's marketplace is perfect for hybrid projects — homeowner posts "need an electrician for 3 days" rather than "full kitchen remodel."

### What Pushes DIY to Professional
- **Code compliance**: Electrical, plumbing, and HVAC work increasingly requires permits and licensed professionals. Mississippi building code enforcement tightened in 2022.
- **Insurance requirements**: Homeowner insurance increasingly requires professional installation for coverage (especially roofing and electrical)
- **Complexity**: YouTube tutorials taught a generation of DIYers, but many discover mid-project that the work is harder than expected. **18% of professional projects** started as DIY attempts that the homeowner abandoned.
- **Time**: Dual-income households have less time for DIY. Paying a professional is a time-value calculation.

## 2027-2029 Growth Projections
- If housing market gains momentum, remodeling poised for stronger growth
- Average growth rate of **4% expected from 2027-2029**
- Cumulative remodeling spend 2026-2029: **~$2.2 trillion**

- Mississippi-specific growth may outpace national average due to infrastructure investment (AWS data center, highway projects) attracting population and driving housing demand
