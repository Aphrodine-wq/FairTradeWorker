# Homeowner Behavior (Survey Data)

## How Homeowners Find Contractors (2026)
1. **Word of mouth:** 74% (still dominant across all age groups)
2. **Repeat business:** 62% (already know a contractor from previous work)
3. **Online search engines:** 54% (Google dominant, Bing/AI search growing)
4. **Social media:** 25% (growing, especially younger buyers)
5. **AI search results:** 11% (new and growing — ChatGPT, Google AI Overviews, Perplexity)
6. **Home service platforms (Angi, Thumbtack):** 18%
7. **Nextdoor / neighborhood apps:** 14%
8. **Hardware store referrals (Home Depot, Lowe's):** 8%

## Generational Differences

### Gen Z / Millennial Homeowners (25-42)
- **48%** start contractor search on their smartphone
- **37%** use social media (Instagram, TikTok, YouTube) to find or vet contractors — watching project videos, before/after content
- **72%** check online reviews before calling ANY contractor, even one referred by a friend
- **61%** prefer to message or text a contractor rather than call
- **44%** have used or would use an app to hire a contractor
- **Higher price sensitivity** but also higher willingness to pay for convenience and transparency
- This group expects an app-like experience: instant quotes, online booking, digital payments
- **FTW's primary homeowner demographic** — they already expect what FTW is building

### Gen X Homeowners (43-58)
- **Hybrid behavior**: comfortable online but still value phone calls and in-person estimates
- **67%** use Google to research contractors but **52%** ultimately hire based on a personal recommendation
- **Most active renovation spenders**: own homes that are 15-25 years old, entering major renovation cycles
- **More likely to have an existing contractor relationship** — FTW needs to be better than their current guy to win them
- Willing to pay for quality and reliability over lowest price
- **55%** use desktop/laptop for research (vs. 48% mobile-first for younger groups)

### Baby Boomer Homeowners (59-77)
- **82%** rely on word of mouth as primary source
- **Only 28%** check online reviews regularly
- **Aging-in-place renovations** are the primary driver: grab bars, walk-in showers, ramps, wider doorways, stair lifts
- Prefer phone calls and in-person interactions
- **Higher average project spend** ($22,000 vs. $15,000 for Millennials) — fewer projects but bigger tickets
- **Less price-sensitive, more quality-sensitive**
- FTW reaches this group through their children (who research online) or through contractor recommendations

## Decision Timeline: From Need to Hire

### Research Phase (1-3 weeks)
- Homeowner identifies a need (leaky roof, outdated kitchen, etc.)
- **78%** of homeowners research online before contacting any contractor
- Average homeowner visits **4-6 websites** during research
- **35%** watch at least one video (YouTube, TikTok) related to their project type
- This phase is longer for discretionary projects (kitchen remodel) and shorter for urgent needs (burst pipe, storm damage)

### Quote Collection (1-2 weeks)
- Homeowners contact an average of **3.2 contractors** for quotes
- **42%** only contact 2 contractors — they hire fast if the first or second feels right
- **28%** contact 4+ contractors — these are the most price-sensitive shoppers
- The contractor who responds fastest wins **40%** of the time, regardless of price
- **Response time is the #1 controllable factor** in winning residential jobs

### Decision Phase (3-7 days)
- After receiving quotes, homeowners decide within **5 business days** on average
- **Factors in order of importance**: (1) trust/reputation, (2) price, (3) timeline, (4) communication quality, (5) professionalism of the estimate
- A well-formatted, professional estimate increases close rate by **23%** vs. a handwritten or basic email quote
- Homeowners who receive a detailed, itemized estimate are **31%** more likely to approve the full scope (vs. lump-sum quotes that trigger "what am I paying for?" anxiety)

### Total Timeline
- **Urgent repairs**: 1-3 days from need to hire
- **Planned maintenance**: 2-4 weeks
- **Discretionary remodels**: 4-12 weeks (often longer — some homeowners research for months)
- **Average across all project types**: ~3 weeks from first search to signed contract

## What Homeowners Care About
- **65%** more likely to call a contractor with **transparent pricing** on their website
- **70%+** would pay more for a contractor with better service reputation
- **Speed and transparent pricing** are the top factors in hiring decisions
- Homeowners expect work to start within **2 weeks** of accepting a quote
- **58%** say a contractor's online presence (website, reviews, photos) matters "a lot" in their decision
- **47%** have eliminated a contractor from consideration based on a poor or nonexistent website

## What Makes Homeowners Abandon a Contractor Search

### Top Abandonment Reasons (in order)
1. **No response or slow response** (43%) — contractor never called back or took 3+ days
2. **Sticker shock without explanation** (31%) — high price with no itemization or justification
3. **Poor reviews or no reviews** (27%) — fewer than 5 reviews, or any review mentioning unfinished work
4. **Unprofessional communication** (24%) — typos in quotes, couldn't explain the scope, rude
5. **No availability for weeks** (22%) — contractor too booked to start within a reasonable window
6. **Couldn't verify license/insurance** (18%) — homeowner searched and couldn't confirm
7. **Negative gut feeling** (15%) — something felt off during the consultation

### The "Ghost Gap"
- **34% of homeowners** report being "ghosted" by a contractor they contacted — no response at all
- This is the single biggest frustration in the hiring process
- Among homeowners who were ghosted, **61%** delayed their project by 30+ days
- **FTW's marketplace eliminates ghosting** by showing contractor availability and enabling instant communication

## Review Behavior

### How Many Reviews Homeowners Read
- **Average: 7-10 reviews** before forming an opinion
- **38%** read the most recent 5 reviews only (recency matters more than volume)
- **22%** read every available review regardless of count
- **Minimum viable review count**: Homeowners skip contractors with fewer than **3 reviews** — they don't trust the sample size

### Rating Thresholds
- **4.5+ stars**: Homeowner proceeds with confidence
- **4.0-4.4 stars**: Homeowner proceeds but reads negative reviews carefully
- **3.5-3.9 stars**: Most homeowners eliminate at this level unless the contractor is a personal referral
- **Below 3.5 stars**: Effectively invisible — homeowner won't consider
- A single **1-star review** doesn't kill a contractor IF they responded professionally and the other reviews are strong
- **Contractor response to negative reviews** matters enormously: **68%** of homeowners say a thoughtful response to criticism increases their trust

### What Homeowners Look For in Reviews
1. Quality of work (did it look good, was it done right?)
2. Communication (did the contractor keep them informed?)
3. Timeline (was it done on time?)
4. Cleanliness (did they respect the home?)
5. Price fairness (was the final bill close to the estimate?)

## Mobile vs. Desktop Behavior

### Search Behavior
- **62% of initial home service searches** happen on mobile devices
- Mobile searches spike on **weekends** (homeowner notices a problem, searches immediately)
- Desktop research peaks on **weekday evenings** (homeowner sits down to compare contractors)
- **Tablet usage**: minimal for search, but **23%** of homeowners review estimates/contracts on tablets

### Conversion Behavior
- Homeowners who find a contractor on mobile are **2.3x more likely** to call or text than fill out a form
- Click-to-call is the dominant mobile conversion action
- Desktop users are more likely to fill out contact forms and compare multiple contractors in tabs
- **Mobile homeowners make faster decisions** but are more likely to hire the first contractor they talk to
- **Desktop homeowners are more methodical** — they compare 3-4 contractors before deciding

### Platform Implications
- FTW's homeowner-facing experience must be mobile-optimized first
- Contractor profiles need to be scannable on a phone screen: rating, availability, price range, and "contact" button above the fold
- Quote/estimate views must render well on mobile — homeowners will review estimates on their phone, not print them

## The Trust Gap
- **30% of homeowners delay renovation projects** because they can't find trusted professionals
- Among those who delay, the average delay is **4.7 months**
- The trust gap is worst for **first-time homeowners** (42% delay) and **homeowners in new cities** (38% delay) who don't have a referral network yet
- **Trust signals that matter most**: verified license, insurance certificate on file, background check badge, and 10+ reviews with 4.5+ rating
- This is FTW's core value prop — verified, background-checked, reviewed contractors with transparent AI-powered estimates
