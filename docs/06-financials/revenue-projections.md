## Sensitivity Analysis

### Pro Conversion Rate Sensitivity

What if the conversion rate from free to Pro varies?

Assuming 500 total contractor signups by end of Year 1:

| Conversion Rate | Pro Contractors | Pro Revenue (monthly at Month 12) | Annual Impact |
|----------------|-----------------|-----------------------------------|---------------|
| 10% | 50 | $2,450 | $29,400 |
| 15% | 75 | $3,675 | $44,100 |
| 20% | 100 | $4,900 | $58,800 |
| 25% | 125 | $6,125 | $73,500 |
| 30% | 150 | $7,350 | $88,200 |

**Key insight:** Every 5% increase in conversion rate adds ~$15K in annual Pro revenue. Conversion rate optimization (better onboarding, clearer Pro value prop, trial extensions) is the highest-leverage growth lever in Year 1.

### Churn Rate Sensitivity

Monthly churn impact on Year 1 ending contractor count (starting with same gross additions):

| Monthly Churn | Active Pro at Month 12 | Monthly Revenue at Month 12 | vs. 5% Baseline |
|--------------|------------------------|----------------------------|-----------------|
| 3% | 58 | $2,842 | +21% |
| 5% | 48 | $2,352 | baseline |
| 8% | 37 | $1,813 | -23% |
| 10% | 31 | $1,519 | -35% |
| 15% | 21 | $1,029 | -56% |

**Key insight:** Churn above 8% monthly makes the model nearly unworkable. At 15% monthly churn, FTW loses more contractors than it gains by Month 8. Keeping churn at or below 5% is existential. Industry SaaS benchmarks: 3-5% monthly for SMB products is typical. Below 3% is excellent.

**Churn reduction strategies:**
- QuickBooks integration creates switching costs (financial data is sticky)
- ConstructionAI estimation accuracy improves with use (personalized to contractor)
- Job history and reviews are non-portable (can't take them to Thumbtack)
- Annual billing discount (pay $470/year instead of $49/month = 20% savings, 0% monthly churn for annual subscribers)

### White-Label Pricing Sensitivity

| Monthly Price | Break-even Clients (to cover $5K salary) | Revenue at 25 Clients |
|--------------|------------------------------------------|----------------------|
| $200/mo | 25 | $5,000/mo |
| $350/mo | 15 | $8,750/mo |
| $500/mo | 10 | $12,500/mo |
| $750/mo | 7 | $18,750/mo |

**Key insight:** White-label pricing has room to increase. MHP is the first client at $350/mo. If FTW delivers strong value, $500-750/mo is justifiable for a branded estimation platform with ConstructionAI.

### Cash Flow Gap Risk

The one cash flow risk: **the gap between SBIR application and disbursement.** If FTW applies for SBIR in Month 3, the review takes 6-9 months, and disbursement takes 30-60 days after award. FTW may not see SBIR money until Month 12-15. During this gap, FTW operates on organic revenue alone.

**Mitigation options:**
1. SBA Microloan ($25-50K) as bridge capital
2. James's personal savings covering 6-12 months of minimal operating costs ($230/month)
3. InvestMS seed investment as earlier-disbursing capital
4. Organic revenue covering costs (achievable in conservative scenario by Month 1)

## Break-Even Analysis

### Monthly Fixed Costs

| Cost | Monthly |
|------|---------|
| Infrastructure (Supabase + Vercel + RunPod) | $50 |
| Insurance (BOP + E&O + Cyber) | $170 |
| Domain + misc | $10 |
| **Total fixed (no salary)** | **$230/mo** |

### Break-Even Points

- **Infrastructure only:** 5 Pro contractors ($245/mo revenue)
- **Infrastructure + insurance:** 5 Pro contractors ($245 > $230)
- **Infrastructure + insurance + 1 salary ($5K/mo):** 107 Pro contractors or 15 white-label clients
- **Infrastructure + insurance + 2 salaries ($10K/mo):** 209 Pro contractors or 29 white-label clients
- **Full team ($25K/mo payroll):** 515 Pro contractors or combination of all streams at ~$25K MRR
- **With SBIR funding ($275K/12mo = $22.9K/mo):** Break-even is covered for 12 months, all revenue is growth

### Margin at Scale

At 200 Pro contractors + 10 white-label:
- Revenue: $13,300/mo
- Infrastructure: ~$500/mo
- Insurance: ~$170/mo
- Checkr (20 new contractors/mo): ~$600/mo
- **Gross margin: ~90%**

At 1,000 Pro contractors + 50 white-label + marketplace:
- Revenue: ~$90,000/mo
- Infrastructure: ~$2,000/mo
- Insurance: ~$300/mo
- Checkr: ~$1,500/mo
- Payment processing: ~$1,000/mo
- **Gross margin: ~95%** (improves at scale because infrastructure costs grow sublinearly)

### Profitability Timeline

| Scenario | Break-Even Month | First Profitable Month (after salaries) |
|----------|-----------------|---------------------------------------|
| Conservative (no SBIR) | Month 1 (costs only) | Month 18 (when revenue covers 1 salary) |
| Conservative (with SBIR) | Month 1 (SBIR covers salaries) | Month 30 (when revenue covers salaries post-SBIR) |
| Moderate | Month 1 | Month 14 |
| Aggressive | Month 1 | Month 10 |

**The bottom line:** FTW is cash-flow positive from day one at the operating level. The question is when revenue is large enough to support a team. With SBIR funding, that bridge is covered. Without SBIR, FTW needs to reach ~$5K MRR before the first hire, which happens around Month 10-12 in the conservative scenario.
