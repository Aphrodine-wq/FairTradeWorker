# Grant & Non-Dilutive Funding Opportunities

## Non-Dilutive Funding Strategy Timeline

Apply in this order to maximize chances and stack funding:

1. **Immediate (Month 1):** Innovate Mississippi MS-FAST Phase 0 — get $3K to fund SBIR proposal writing
2. **Month 1-2:** SSBCI 2.0 / InvestMS — apply for seed-stage funding while writing SBIR
3. **Month 2-3:** NSF SBIR Phase I — submit ConstructionAI proposal
4. **Month 3-4:** USDA SBIR Phase I — submit rural market proposal (can apply simultaneously with NSF)
5. **Month 4-6:** USDA Rural Business Development Grant — apply while SBIR is under review
6. **Month 6-8:** ConnectMS CoBuilders Accelerator — apply for next cohort
7. **Month 8-12:** Microsoft for Startups — apply for Azure credits to reduce hosting costs
8. **Month 12-18:** NSF SBIR Phase II — if Phase I is awarded
9. **Month 18+:** Strategic Breakthrough track — if Phase II succeeds and ConstructionAI has traction

**Can you apply to NSF and USDA SBIR simultaneously?** Yes. They are different federal agencies with separate SBIR programs. You can hold awards from both as long as the proposed work is distinct (different research objectives). FTW strategy: NSF proposal focuses on the AI/ML innovation (ConstructionAI model architecture), USDA proposal focuses on the rural market application (serving underserved rural contractors).

## Tier 1: High Fit (Apply Now)

### SBIR Phase I — NSF Artificial Intelligence Topic

- **Amount:** Up to $275,000 (Phase I, 12 months) / $2,000,000 (Phase II, 24 months)
- **Agency:** National Science Foundation (NSF)
- **Program URL:** https://seedfund.nsf.gov/
- **Current solicitation:** NSF 24-582 (check for updates — solicitations refresh annually)
- **Topic:** Small Business ERC / AI & Intelligent Systems
- **Department to contact:** NSF Division of Industrial Innovation and Partnerships (IIP)
- **Program officer:** Check nsf.gov/staff for current SBIR program directors in the AI topic area. As of 2025, the AI/ML topic is managed through the Computer and Information Science and Engineering (CISE) directorate.

**Why FTW fits:**
- ConstructionAI is a novel fine-tuned AI model (Llama 3.1 8B) for construction estimation
- 5,200+ domain-specific training examples is genuine research-grade data curation
- 8 specialized tools integrated into the model
- No existing construction-specific LLM at this level of domain expertise
- Addresses real economic impact (construction is 4% of GDP, estimation errors cost billions)

**Application requirements checklist:**
- [ ] Company must be US-based, for-profit, <500 employees
- [ ] Principal Investigator (PI) must be primarily employed by the company (51%+ time)
- [ ] PI must be a US citizen or permanent resident
- [ ] Project summary (1 page): problem, solution, broader impact
- [ ] Project description (15 pages max): technical approach, innovation, team, commercialization plan
- [ ] Budget and budget justification
- [ ] Biographical sketches (NSF format) for all senior personnel
- [ ] Current and pending support documentation
- [ ] Data management plan
- [ ] Letters of support from potential customers (Josh at MHP, Oxford contractors)
- [ ] Postmark deadline: check seedfund.nsf.gov for next submission window

**Tips for strengthening FTW's application:**
- Emphasize the novel training data pipeline (how 5,200 construction examples were curated and validated)
- Highlight the 8-tool integration as a novel approach to domain-specific LLM augmentation
- Include pilot data: estimation accuracy metrics from ConstructionAI vs. manual estimates
- Show market validation: Josh at MHP as a paying customer, Oxford contractor interest
- Frame broader impact: cost transparency reduces homeowner exploitation, supports small businesses
- Include a clear Phase II vision (multi-trade expansion, regional pricing models, accuracy feedback loop)
- Use Innovate Mississippi's SBIR proposal assistance (they have experience with NSF applications)

**What previous winners look like:**
- Typically pre-revenue or early-revenue stage ($0-500K ARR)
- Team of 1-5 people
- Clear technical innovation (not just a business model)
- Strong PI with relevant domain expertise (James's construction background is an asset)
- NSF success rate: ~25% for Phase I (competitive but achievable)

**Realistic probability of winning:** 20-30% for a well-written first application. Increases to 35-45% if resubmitted with reviewer feedback.

**Timeline:** 6-12 months from submission to award notification. Funds disbursed over 12 months.

**New in 2026:** "Strategic Breakthrough" track provides up to $30M for post-Phase II commercialization. This is a long-term target if ConstructionAI gains significant traction.

- [ ] Company registration with Innovate Mississippi

**Tips:**
- Apply immediately — this is free money to fund the NSF SBIR proposal
- Use the $3K primarily for professional SBIR proposal review/editing services
- Innovate Mississippi staff can connect FTW with experienced SBIR proposal writers

### SSBCI 2.0 / InvestMS for Startups

- **Amount:** Up to $11M pool for Pre-Seed, Seed, and Series A startups in Mississippi
- **Program URL:** https://innovate.ms/investms/
- **Contact:** Innovate Mississippi, Capital Programs team
- **Administered by:** Innovate Mississippi on behalf of Mississippi Development Authority (MDA)
- **Federal source:** State Small Business Credit Initiative (SSBCI) 2.0, US Treasury

**FTW's fit:**
- Pre-seed/seed stage Mississippi tech startup
- Technology-based (AI/ML)
- Located in a target county (Lafayette County)
- Job creation potential in Mississippi

**How InvestMS works:**
- Functions as an equity investment program, not a grant
- State invests alongside private investors (1:1 match or better)
- Investment sizes: $50K-$500K typical for pre-seed/seed
- Requires FTW to raise some private capital that InvestMS matches
- Terms are startup-friendly (Mississippi wants to grow its tech ecosystem)

**Mississippi's total SSBCI allocation:** $86M across all programs (venture capital, loan participation, collateral support, equity co-investment). The InvestMS startup pool is ~$11M of this.

**Application requirements:**
- [ ] Company incorporated in Mississippi or willing to relocate primary operations
- [ ] Business plan or pitch deck
- [ ] Financial projections (3-5 year)
- [ ] Team bios
- [ ] Description of technology and market opportunity
- [ ] Committed or prospective private co-investor

**Tips:**
- Innovate Mississippi is eager to deploy this capital — they have a mandate to invest in MS tech startups
- Having a working product (FTW frontend + ConstructionAI) puts FTW ahead of most applicants
- Josh at MHP as a paying customer is strong validation
- Frame FTW as a Mississippi job creator (market reps, customer success, eventually engineering)

**Realistic probability:** 40-50% (competitive but FTW has a strong profile for this program)

- **Amount:** Up to $50,000

- **Interest rate:** 8-13% (higher than traditional loans but available to startups with no revenue)
- **Term:** Up to 6 years
- **Mississippi intermediaries:**

**Note:** These are loans, not grants. Must be repaid. Use only if SBIR is awarded and you need bridge capital during the disbursement lag.

## Funding Stack Strategy

The goal is to stack non-dilutive funding to reach profitability without giving up equity:

| Source | Amount | Type | Timeline | Probability |
|--------|--------|------|----------|-------------|
| MS-FAST Phase 0 | $3,000 | Reimbursement | Month 1-2 | 80% |
| NSF SBIR Phase I | $275,000 | Grant | Month 8-14 | 25% |
| USDA SBIR Phase I | $275,000 | Grant | Month 10-16 | 30% |
| USDA RBDG | $100,000 | Grant | Month 6-12 | 25% |
| InvestMS (SSBCI) | $150,000 | Equity co-invest | Month 4-8 | 40% |
| Microsoft for Startups | $150,000 | Credits | Month 3-6 | 65% |
| SBA Microloan | $50,000 | Loan | Month 2-4 | 70% |
| **Expected value** | **~$350,000** | | | |
| **Maximum if all hit** | **$1,003,000** | | | |

Expected value calculation: sum of (amount x probability) for each source. Realistically, FTW should plan on receiving $250K-400K in non-dilutive funding over the first 18 months, with SBIR Phase I being the single largest and most impactful award.

If SBIR Phase I is awarded, the path to Phase II ($2M) opens up. Phase II + organic revenue could fund FTW through Year 3 without any equity dilution.
